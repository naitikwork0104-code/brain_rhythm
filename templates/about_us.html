NeuroResonance database developed by Dr. Pushpendra Singh during his doctoral studies at Research Center for Materials Nanoarchitectonics (MANA) at NIMS, Japan from 2016 to 2021.

This database serves as a comprehensive repository of neural response patterns to various nanomaterial stimuli, offering unprecedented insights into brain-material interactions. Dr. Singh now continues to expand and refine this work under the guidance of Prof. Anirban Bandyopadhyay.

Chronology of Database

21 August 2021

NIMS, Japan launched the NeuroResonance Database, establishing the first structured repository of rhythm-engineered neural response data.

1 October 2024

IIT Mandi, India joined the database initiative, expanding the scope to include applied neuro-engineering and biomedical rhythm studies.

March 2025

  • March 15, 2025: Sapiotectronics Lab, IIT Mandi restructured the entire NeuroResonance Database.
  • March 31, 2025: Published research works related to DDG were updated.
